Stamps of Australia

How One Country Evolved Philatourism - all International Stamps Are Now Designed to Attract Tourists

Early Australian stamps had little focus on use to attract tourists. This stamp from 1936 is one of the earlier stamps to show land and natural beauty. Prior to this, most stamps were stylized Kangaroos, pictures of royalty or various commemorations of history. Even this stamp, while different from previous stamps, focused on commemoration.

Source:StampWorld.com

With improvements in color on stamps, more diversity was possible. This stamp, one of a set of four, focused on scenes throughout Australia. This stamp shows the famous Mount Ayers while oters showed Richmond Bridge, Broken Bay, and Mount Buffalo.

Source:StampWorld.com

Adding true photgraphs to stamps was a major set forward. This 1976 stamp depicts Royal National Park in New South Wales, one of a set of 3 National Park stamps. While likely targeted at domestic tourists, stamps such as these brought new venues into evreyday focus.

Source:StampWorld.com

In 2000, Australia began a series of stamps for use in International Post. These were clearly aimed at selling the country to foreign tourists. About 30 different International Post stamps were issued between 2000 and today.

Source:StampWorld.com

While Australia contiues to produce stamps which show the beauty of the country, as seen in this 2012 stamp,there is an attempt to appeal to other interests besides natural beauty. history, cities and gardens have alos appeared on the International Post stamps

Source:StampWorld.com
